 1. Explainer moves before the output reveal — Steps 7 (sample plan) and 8 (transition video): added notes that Harish's
  recommendation was to show the explanatory content before the sample plan/output, not after, to improve interpretation and reduce
  drop-off.
  2. Email delivery replaces device download as the primary path — Steps 24, 28, 29: retitled/reframed from "download FP..." to
  "in-app summary + emailed PDF," per the team's decision to avoid handing users a reason to exit the app.
  3. New pre-plan / post-plan messaging requirement — Step 22 (full picture, pre-paywall): added the requirement for a contextual
  post-plan messaging beat (distinct from Step 7's generic pre-plan one), with format (video/image/text) deliberately left open for
  funnel A/B testing.
  4. Tier content granularity made explicit — Tier legend + Step 29: Tier 1 = amounts only, no scheme/product names; Tier 2 = named
  products/allocations; Tier 3 = full-service, candidate for on-platform execution, RIA-fee-vs-distribution-commission model still
  open.
  5. Investment Plan must conform to Financial Plan's allocation — Recommendation Engine system card: added as an explicit
  constraint, since it was only an assumed dependency before.
  6. AA consent enriched with competitor benchmark (Investify/FINVU demo) — Step 27: single-OTP multi-account discovery, consent
  parameters (1-month storage, 12-month validity, 45 fetches/month), plus a QA flag on collapsed holdings and risk-profile
  misclassification observed in the demo.
  7. Compliance still open — flagged, not resolved — Steps 23 and 27: added "pending Megha/Piali review" notes for whether signing
  must precede AA data collection and whether FIU/IU licensing is required.
  8. Direct stocks must not be positioned as superior to mutual funds — Curated product shelf system card: added as a compliance
  guardrail on recommendation copy.
  9. Tier-3 billing cadence (quarterly vs. annual) — undecided — Step 23: noted alongside the existing "price TBC" flag, with a lean
  toward upfront value for a one-time payment.
