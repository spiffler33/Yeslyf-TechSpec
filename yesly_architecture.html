Architecture

A broad technical architecture for Yesly — the mobile-first conscious money platform from House of Alpha. This is the source document for development partner scoping, quoting, and build.

How to read this document

This document and the thirteen flowcharts that follow are a single integrated artefact. The architecture describes what the system is. The flowcharts describe what the system does. A development partner cannot quote against one without the other. Read this overview first, then the charts in any order — they are cross-referenced throughout.

What Yesly Is

  • A mobile-first conscious money platform for Indian earners aged 25–45.
  • A financial planning and advisory funnel delivered through community, planning, recommendations, and human advisor handoff.
  • A four-tier monetised product: Free → Financial Plan (one-time) → Investment Plan (one-time) → Ongoing Subscription (monthly) → Human Advisory (off-app).
  • A read-consumer of users' financial data via Account Aggregator consent, with CAS and statement uploads as fallback.
  • A SEBI RIA-compliant advisory channel routing qualified users to House of Alpha advisors.
  • An execution-initiating platform that places trades on behalf of subscribed users via smallcase Gateway and BSE StAR MF. Yesly is not the broker of record — smallcase and BSE StAR are.
  • An internally-developed algorithmic core: asset allocation logic, rebalancing triggers, and (v2) idea generation are built in-house by the HoA engine team. The Investment Plan engine is owned internally, not by the development partner.

What Yesly Is Not

  • Not a broker. Order routing happens via licensed third parties (smallcase, BSE StAR).
  • Not a wallet or custodian. Yesly does not hold or transfer user money beyond its own subscription billing.
  • Not a card issuer.
  • Not a lending platform.
  • Not on UPI peer-to-peer rails. UPI is used only as a subscription mandate channel via the payment gateway.
  • Not a market-data terminal. Live prices and quotes are not a feature.
  • Not PCI-DSS in scope. Card data is fully tokenised at gateway and never touches Yesly servers.
Cost implication for partner

This scope eliminates approximately 40–60% of a generic Indian-fintech reference architecture. The development partner must quote against this scope, not against a generic UPI-rails-and-cards diagram. Any line item in their quote that assumes wallet, ledger, card, or UPI P2P infrastructure should be challenged.

Compliance Posture

Yesly operates as a digital channel for a SEBI-registered Investment Adviser (House of Alpha). The architecture must:

  • Enforce the SEBI RIA advertisement code (no return promises, no testimonials, mandatory disclosures shown and logged).
  • Maintain immutable audit logs of every advice-bearing interaction for five years from the end of the client relationship.
  • Support DPDP Act 2023 data subject rights — access, correction, erasure — within stipulated timelines.
  • Resolve the SEBI-vs-DPDP retention conflict by anonymising data on erasure requests rather than deleting it (audit and advisory records retained in anonymised form).
  • Store all personal data in Indian regions (ap-south-1).
  • Delegate KYC to a licensed third-party vendor, triggered at Tier 2 entry (Investment Plan).
  • Operate under VAPT clearance before launch and annually thereafter.

  • Pledge mechanic. Optional, not a gate. The pledge_taken flag drives a visible badge inside the community. Users who decline can pledge later.

Confidence flags

AA path: high · CAS PDF parsing: moderate (NSDL CAS covers NSDL + CDSL + most MFs; bank balances not included) · Bank/card statement parsing: moderate (PDF formats vary across banks; OCR may be needed) · Manual entry: low (functionally possible but UX-heavy; users will abandon).

  • Three DPDP rights + grievance = four user-initiated surfaces. All four must exist in v1. Skipping any is a regulatory exposure.
  • Erasure resolution = anonymise. Hard-delete would violate SEBI's 5-yr retention. Anonymise-don't-delete is the industry-standard reconciliation.
  • 30-day cooling-off on erasure prevents impulse deletion and aligns with DPDP's "right to withdraw."
  • Grievance SLA enforcement is automated. Escalations trigger via cron, not human chasing. SEBI auditors will check this.
  • SEBI SCORES submission is manual. HoA compliance team submits — Yesly exports the data.
  • All four flows write to the immutable audit trail per Chart 10.
